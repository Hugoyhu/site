    • Photo Editing Policy

    Photography is an art, and different photographers have their own opinions on where to draw the line for editing. This page explains the processes I do (or don't) use and a brief explanation why. These opinions are my own and shouldn't be taken as criticism for others' work. Art is subjective.

    Editing Software

    I use DxO PhotoLab 7 and ViewPoint 4. Previously, I've used Darktable, but all images have been re-edited in PhotoLab.

    Colors and Lighting

    Images are usually touched up to make them closer to what they look like in real life.

    White Balance

    I shoot RAW + JPG, so I have the ability to modify the WB of my images when editing RAW. I do this occasionally- it allows me to focus getting the shot, not fiddling with settings.

    Noise Reduction

    DxO DEEPPRIME XD is used to reduce noise in images, especially handheld nighttime shots that require higher shutter speeds. DEEPRIME XD uses machine learning. This provides de-noising to provide stellar shots even at high ISOs.

    Generative AI (and any other AI, excluding Denoise)

    Never used.

    Optical Corrections

    Lens Distortion and vignetting is corrected with DxO PhotoLab.
